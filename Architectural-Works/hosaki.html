Works

hosaki

Fall 2025 | Advanced Design

Professor Nanako Umemoto & Billy Gargroetzi

This project proposes rethinking Omishima Island in Japan through architecture that addresses the island's damaged landscape, aging population, and social growth. Inspired by the Scout Jamboree, it explores hand-built, pod-based dwellings located on Point Toyo, a former terraced mandarin grove now overgrown and without infrastructure. The project responds to remoteness while aiming to bring new people to the island. The goal is to create a new model of collective living that connects people to nature and resources.

The matcha whisk, or the Chasen, is a small piece of architecture, delicately carved from a piece of bamboo, yet multiplied into dozens of slender tines that bloom outward. Each hosaki curves with quiet intention, carrying the memory of the craft in its grain. Like the matcha whisk, this same principle breathes into my superstructure.
